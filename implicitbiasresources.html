What is Implicit Bias?

Implicit bias is a term that refers to a person's unconscious automatic associations, stereotypes, and system 1 processing. These biases, often taught subconsciously by a society, are how we as humans make quick decisions and opinions on people even if these initial feelings are not what our values would reflect.

An implicit bias test, as run by Harvard University, is a tool to measure this implicit bias by presenting the test taker with a set of people and words and measuring how strong certain associations 'click' in comparison to others. Overall this test, with its quick timing and easy controls, aims to reveal the unconscious implicit biases a person might have when making automatic split decisions, and with a variety of different tests, it can provide a test taker a comprehensive overview of where their biases are stronger and weaker.
